Afrostream: Europe's First African Content SVOD

2015-2017 | Co-Founder & CTO

‚Ç¨4M Total Raised
28 Countries
12M Households Reach

The Context

In 2015, we identified a massive underserved market: 150M+ Africans and African diaspora with no dedicated premium streaming service for African content. We decided to build the "Netflix for African content."

The Challenge

  • Build a full SVOD platform from scratch with limited funding
  • Secure content licenses from major Hollywood and Nollywood studios
  • Deploy across 28 countries with varying internet infrastructure
  • Compete with global giants entering the market

The Solution

As CTO, I built the entire technical stack and led fundraising alongside our CEO. Key achievements:

  • Accepted into Y Combinator Summer 2015 batch
  • Secured licenses from Lionsgate, MGM, and major Nollywood producers
  • Built partnerships with Orange, Canal+, and Bouygues Telecom
  • Developed adaptive streaming for low-bandwidth African markets

The Results

  • ‚Ç¨4M raised from VCs and strategic investors
  • Y Combinator S15 acceptance (top 2% of applicants)
  • 12M households reach via telco partnerships
  • 28 countries across Europe and Africa

Key Learnings

While Afrostream eventually closed due to market timing and competition from global players, the experience provided invaluable lessons in startup building, fundraising, and scaling technology in emerging markets.
